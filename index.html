Who We Are

Built for students.
Driven by ambition.

The Intellectuals Savings Group is a member-governed savings collective founded to foster financial discipline and mutual support among students. Every shilling saved here builds a stronger future for the whole family.


We operate with full transparency — every contribution is recorded, every loan is approved by the Finance Committee, and every cycle ends with a full disbursement and financial review.

The Process

How it works

01

Register & Onboard

Pay your one-time membership fee of UGX 5,000, contribute UGX 5,000 to the Capital Reserve Fund, and purchase your savings booklet for UGX 2,000. You're officially an Intellectual.

02

Save Weekly

Every Wednesday at 4:30 PM, attend the savings meeting and contribute a minimum of UGX 1,000. Savings are recorded in your booklet and master ledger simultaneously.

03

Access Loans

Apply to borrow up to 75% of your total savings. The Finance Committee reviews your request, and upon the President's approval, funds are disbursed within 48 hours.

04

Stay Accountable

Punctuality, attendance, and contribution consistency are monitored. Fines keep the group disciplined and funds growing. The Disciplinary Committee handles any breaches.

05

Attend GMs

Two mandatory General Meetings per cycle — one to set semester goals, one to review performance and distribute funds to all members.

06

Collect at Cycle End

At the close of each 16-week cycle, all savings (minus fines and outstanding debts) are paid out. Your money, grown with discipline.

Savings Rules at a Glance

  • Minimum contribution of UGX 1,000 every Wednesday at 4:30 PM.
  • Late arrival after 4:35 PM incurs a UGX 500 fine; significant lateness costs UGX 1,000.
  • Unexcused absence: UGX 2,000 fine plus the weekly savings still owed.
  • Absentee members still owe the weekly minimum unless excused by the group.
  • Loans require a written application to the Finance Committee for appraisal.
  • Early withdrawal requires 2-week notice (except verified emergencies).
  • Full savings paid out at the end of each cycle, minus outstanding fines or debts.
